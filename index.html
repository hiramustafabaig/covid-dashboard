Data-Driven Insights

Key Discoveries

Advanced analytics revealing critical patterns in pandemic data

Exponential First Wave

Global confirmed cases grew from near zero in January to 19.2 million by late July 2020 — doubling roughly every 3 weeks at peak velocity.

Peak Daily New Cases (Jul 27) ~230,000

Fatality Rate Disparities

Case fatality rates range from under 0.5% to 15.5% across countries — heavily influenced by testing coverage rather than healthcare quality alone.

Global Fatality Rate ~3.7%

Testing Inequality

Testing capacity varied by over 1,000x between nations. Countries with low tests/million show inflated fatality rates — more cases undetected, not more deaths.

Max Tests per Million >300,000

Americas Dominance

North and South America together accounted for over 55% of all global cases by July 2020, driven by the USA (5M) and Brazil (2.9M) outbreaks.

Americas Share of Cases ~55%

Deaths vs Cases Correlation

Deaths and confirmed cases show near-perfect Pearson correlation (r ≈ 0.97) globally, confirming that case burden is the strongest predictor of mortality count.

Pearson r (Deaths vs Cases) ~0.97

Global Recovery Rate

Despite the scale of the outbreak, 63% of confirmed cases recovered by July 2020. Asia and Oceania showed the highest recovery rates proportionally.

Global Recovery Rate 63%
